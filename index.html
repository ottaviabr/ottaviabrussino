Ottavia Brussino

I work in social impact, focused on building connections that promote more inclusive and sustainable systems. Alongside this, I'm passionate about photography as a way to explore and reveal the ties between different cultures and experiences. I travel with my camera in hand, and I am passionate about curating photojournalism projects that share powerful and inspiring stories, bringing fresh perspectives from around the world.

my work

I often travel, sometimes for work, sometimes out of curiosity. I enjoy discovering new places and cultures, and my camera usually comes with me. It helps me hold on to what I see and what I feel. My photography captures people within their environments and how they inhabit and move through their landscapes and places. I prefer to document these relationships between people and place without focusing on individual faces, looking instead for moments that speak to something more universal. This approach also reflects a commitment to ethical photography, especially in contexts where people's stories deserve care, not exposure. Whether documenting impact projects or wandering new streets, I use the lens to explore connection, memory, and emotion.

curated projects

I've curated storytelling projects that use photography to bridge local realities with wider audiences. What draws me in is the chance to connect different worlds, using images and narrative to shift perspective, spark dialogue, or carry a story further than it might have gone on its own.
Through Our Eyes, a global campaign of kid‑led storytelling from a refugee camp in Greece.
Yhdessä (Together): A digital storytelling photodocumentary on inclusion and friendship in Finnish schools.
